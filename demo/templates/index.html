The Canonizer

This tool takes the text of a novel and classifies it as either canonical or non-canonical based on its word frequencies, using machine learning. For the purposes of this tool, a novel is considered canonical when DBNL (a digital archive of Dutch literature) has one or more secondary references to it (i.e., a reviewer or literary scholar discusses the novel in a secondary text that is part of DBNL). The system has been trained on a corpus of 1346 novels from 1800-2000 available in DBNL and is able to distinguish canonical and non-canonical texts with a crossvalidated accuracy score of 72%. Results may be substantially less reliable or meaningful for texts from different genres and periods. This tool serves to explore the learned association between textual features and canonicity and is therefore intended purely for academic purposes. In particular, distant reading, as performed by this tool, is no substitute for actual reading!
